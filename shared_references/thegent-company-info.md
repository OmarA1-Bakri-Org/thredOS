# About TheGent

TheGent (thegent.uk) is a cloud-native settlement platform enabling instant cross-border payments. It is a SaaS solution that banks, PSPs, and money transfer operators integrate via a single API or UI.

## What It Does

The platform bypasses SWIFT and correspondent banking networks entirely. Instead of routing payments through chains of intermediary banks (each taking a cut and adding delay), TheGent connects members directly. Transactions settle in seconds, 24/7 including weekends and holidays. There is no pre-funding requirement — members don't need to park capital in nostro accounts across the network.

## How It Works

Members connect via a single API or web UI. The platform handles routing, FX, and settlement across the member network. Smart liquidity management optimises cash positioning across currency corridors without requiring upfront capital reserves. The platform supports 30+ currencies.

## Key Differentiators vs Traditional Infrastructure

- **No correspondent banking chain** — direct member-to-member settlement, eliminating intermediary fees and delays
- **No pre-funding** — no nostro/vostro account requirements, freeing up trapped capital
- **Real-time, 24/7** — settles in seconds, not business days. Weekends and holidays included
- **Smart liquidity management** — optimises FX and cash positioning across corridors automatically
- **Competitive FX** — access to FX rates across the member network, not just one provider
- **Fast integration** — sandbox in minutes, production in days. Not months or quarters
- **30+ currencies** — and expanding. New corridors can be added without new integrations

## vs SWIFT / Correspondent Banking

SWIFT is a messaging network — it tells banks to move money, but the actual settlement happens through chains of correspondent banks. This means pre-funded nostro accounts, cut-off times, multi-day settlement, and opaque fees. TheGent replaces this entire chain with direct settlement.

## vs Fintechs (Wise, Airwallex, Thunes)

Most cross-border fintechs still rely on correspondent banking or local payment rails underneath. They've improved the user experience but not the underlying infrastructure. TheGent operates at the infrastructure layer — it's what fintechs and banks can build ON, not compete with.

## The FC / Participant Model

- **Funds Controllers (FC)**: Licensed entities (PSPs, EMIs, banks, exchanges) that hold and move funds. They integrate with TheGent to settle directly with other FCs
- **Participants**: Companies that connect to the network for reach (corridor access, FX) but don't hold/move funds themselves. They transact through an FC
