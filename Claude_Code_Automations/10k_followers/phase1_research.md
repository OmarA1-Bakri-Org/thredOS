## Topic 1: Profile Optimisation

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/linkedin + ruben.substack.com/p/from-49-to-10000-followers-in-17
- **Core Claim**: A fully optimised profile converts profile visitors into followers; it takes 5 minutes to set up once and never needs revisiting.
- **Mechanism**: LinkedIn's algorithm surfaces profiles through search and recommendations. A clear, visually distinct profile with a focused mission statement creates instant recognition and a reason to follow. The profile acts as a landing page — every element must answer "why should I follow this person?"
- **Action Steps**:
  1. **Profile Picture**: HD 1080x1080 px. Zoom until face fills the frame, removing background clutter. Smile noticeably. Own a distinct, consistent colour for instant recognition in the feed.
  2. **Banner**: HD 1584x396 px. Single clear message that reinforces your mission. LinkedIn Premium enables slideshow banners.
  3. **Headline**: Make it cheeky and clear. Reflects your mission, not your job title. Example: "Master AI before it masters you" (not "AI Consultant at XYZ Corp").
  4. **Featured Section**: One link only, big text, no descriptions. Direct clicks matter more than explanations.
  5. **About Section**: Keep it minimal. Extensive experience sections rank higher in LinkedIn search than long About bios.
  6. **Premium Upgrade**: Enables a special link placement under your name for improved conversion rates.
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: LinkedIn Premium (for banner slideshow + link placement)
- **Quantified Proof**: Maria Zhanette's account: 37 connections / 49 followers on Nov 18, 2025 → 10,000+ followers by early Dec 2025.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topic 3 (Mission) to write headline and banner text.

## Topic 4: Finding Your "One" Reader (ICP Definition)

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/from-49-to-10000-followers-in-17
- **Core Claim**: Target ONE specific person (your ICP), not "everyone." Every post should feel like it was written for that one person.
- **Mechanism**: Psychological — when content feels personally relevant, readers engage more (comments, shares, follows). Algorithmically — high engagement signals tell LinkedIn to distribute the post further. Specificity creates resonance; generality creates scrolling.
- **Action Steps**:
  1. Use ICP-GPT (Topic 6) or manually define your ideal reader.
  2. Create a detailed persona: name, age, gender, job title, location, specific struggle, aspiration.
  3. Example: "Sophia, 40, female, independent management consultant in Europe, wants to position herself as an AI-upskilling expert."
  4. Before every post, ask: "Would Sophia stop scrolling for this?"
  5. Write as if you're texting Sophia, not broadcasting to thousands.
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: ICP-GPT (Topic 6)
- **Quantified Proof**: None directly cited. Indirectly validated by the 10K-in-17-days result.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topic 3 (Mission) to know what problem you're solving before identifying who has that problem.

## Topic 5: #1 Rule for LinkedIn Content

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/from-49-to-10000-followers-in-17 + ruben.substack.com/p/1000000
- **Core Claim**: Write for your reader, not about yourself. "I (still) never write a personal post. I know no one cares about me."
- **Mechanism**: LinkedIn's algorithm rewards engagement (comments, shares, dwell time). Posts about the reader's problems generate more engagement than posts about the creator's achievements. The reader must see themselves in every post.
- **Action Steps**:
  1. Frame every post around a reader problem, not your accomplishment.
  2. Use Claude with this prompt: "Find the best angle/topic for one LinkedIn post that turns my expertise into something useful, relatable, and 100% about the reader — not bragging — by asking clarifying questions about real problems my audience struggles with, what expertise feels most useful to strangers, and how to frame it so readers see themselves."
  3. Test: if you removed your name from the post, would the reader still find value? If not, rewrite.
  4. Avoid: personal stories, "I achieved X" framing, humble brags.
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: Claude AI (for content angle development)
- **Quantified Proof**: 344,258 followers gained in 2025 alone using this rule.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topic 4 (ICP) to know whose problems you're writing about.

  2. **30 min — Community Engagement** (the most important block):
     - Comment on 10-15 posts BEFORE publishing your own post (warm up)

  3. **10 min — Direct Outreach**:
     - Respond to all DMs promptly
     - Send 5 targeted connection requests daily
     - Use LinkedIn filters: 2nd connections, location, following status, job title
  4. **Never miss a day**. "I never missed a day in 3 years."
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: Grok (Reddit research), Canva (design), Nano Banana (image editing), Claude AI (copywriting)

## Topic 8: Driving Comments on Posts

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/1000000 + LinkedIn posts
- **Core Claim**: Comments are the #1 engagement signal on LinkedIn. More comments = more algorithmic distribution = more followers.
- **Mechanism**: LinkedIn's algorithm weighs comments heavily in distribution decisions. Comments also create social proof — a post with 50 comments attracts more clicks than one with 5. The "warm up" strategy primes the algorithm to show your post to more people.
- **Action Steps**:
  1. End every post with a low-friction question. Not "What do you think?" but specific: "What's the one AI tool you use daily?"
  2. Warm up your account with 10-15 comments on others' posts BEFORE publishing.
  3. Reply to every comment on your post within the first 2 hours.
  4. Create "open loops" in hooks that demand resolution in comments.
  5. Use the carousel TL;DR slide with a call-to-action question (Slide 10 of carousel format).
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: None specific — this is a behavioural technique.
- **Quantified Proof**: 189K impressions and ~1K new followers in a single day using carousel + comment strategy.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topic 5 (content rule) — reader-focused content naturally generates more comments.

## Topic 9: Best Viral Post Format

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/1000000 + ruben.substack.com/p/carousels
- **Core Claim**: The optimal LinkedIn post format is a single 1080x1350 image + 2-line hook + short body text. Carousels (PDF, 6-14 slides, 1080x1350) are the highest-performing format for growth.
- **Mechanism**: The image takes 80% of the screen, the hook 20%. This visual dominance stops scrolling. Carousels force multiple swipes, increasing dwell time — a key algorithm signal.
- **Action Steps**:
  **Single Image Post:**
  1. Create a 1080x1350 (4:5 aspect ratio) single image.
  2. Make it look scrappy and unbranded — never like an ad.
  3. Include useful, bookmarkable content (infographic, cheat sheet, checklist).
  4. Write a 2-line hook (~55 characters each) using pattern interrupt techniques.
  5. Body text: short, about the reader, no hashtags, no emojis.

  **Carousel Post:**
  1. Create a PDF, 6-14 slides at 1080x1350.
  2. Slide 1: Polarising/curiosity-gap headline (hook).
  3. Slide 2: Stakes — why this matters now.
  4. Slide 3: Core insight or golden quote.
  5. Slides 4-8: Value breakdown — one insight per slide.
  6. Slide 9: TL;DR checklist.
  7. Slide 10: Low-friction CTA question.
  8. Design in Gamma.app (Studio Mode, Social format, Portrait, "Just vibes" text).

  **Hook Techniques:**
  - Contradiction: "The worst LinkedIn posts get the most followers"
  - Specific number: "I mass-unfollowed 2,000 people. My engagement tripled"
  - Direct accusation: "You're writing for your mom, not your audience"
  - Stolen thought: Express what readers secretly think
  - Absurd reframe: Make mundane topics dramatic
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: Gamma.app (carousel design), Gemini (content extraction)
- **Quantified Proof**: 189K impressions, ~1K followers in one day from a single carousel.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topics 3-5 (mission + ICP + content rule).

## Topic 10: Pinterest for LinkedIn Images

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/1000000 + ruben.substack.com/p/imagine
- **Core Claim**: Pinterest is the best source of visual inspiration for LinkedIn content. Don't create from scratch — find references and modify.
- **Mechanism**: Pinterest curates high-quality visual styles. By finding an existing infographic or visual style, you bypass the "blank canvas" problem and produce better designs faster.
- **Action Steps**:
  1. Go to Pinterest.
  2. Search: `[your niche] + "graph" OR "cheat sheet" OR "infographic" OR "handwritten style infographic"`.
  3. Find a visual style you like (layout, colour scheme, typography).
  4. Upload the reference image to Gemini.
  5. Prompt Gemini: "Extract all information from this infographic so another designer could remake it."
  6. Find a second Pinterest image with your desired visual style (e.g., handwritten style).
  7. Prompt Gemini: "Remake this infographic with [extracted content from step 5] but in this style [uploaded image from step 6]."
  8. Colour-correct in Canva to match your brand palette.
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**: Pinterest (inspiration), Google Gemini (extraction + generation), Canva (colour correction)
- **Quantified Proof**: None specific to this technique in isolation.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topic 9 (format) for dimensions and style guidelines.

## Topic 11: Removing "AI Image" Tag

- **Source URL**: ruben.substack.com/p/from-49-to-10000-followers-in-17 (paywalled section)
- **Core Claim**: AI-generated images uploaded to LinkedIn may display an "AI-generated" tag. This tag can be removed by stripping image metadata before uploading.
- **Mechanism**: AI image generators (Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.) embed C2PA content credentials and generative AI metadata in image files. LinkedIn reads this metadata and labels the image. Stripping it removes the label.
- **Action Steps**:
  1. Generate your image in your tool of choice.
  2. Use an AI metadata removal tool (e.g., aimetadatacleaner.com or exifreader.com/ai-metadata-remover).
  3. Upload the cleaned image.
  4. Alternative: take a screenshot of the image instead of downloading — screenshots don't carry generation metadata.
  5. Alternative: open in an image editor, export as new file (strips metadata).
- **Tools/Resources Referenced**:
  - aimetadatacleaner.com (free online tool)
  - exifreader.com/ai-metadata-remover (free online tool)
  - Screenshot method (no tool needed)
- **Quantified Proof**: None cited.
- **Dependency**: Requires Topic 2 or 10 (AI image generation workflow).
- **Note**: Specific details are in the paywalled section. The metadata removal approach was reconstructed from web search results about AI image tagging on LinkedIn.
